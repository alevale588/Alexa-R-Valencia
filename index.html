My previous career experiences have been limited to fast food and retail as of the past few years. Due to the pandemic, I have not yet been able to fully immerse into opportunities such as internships and careers in my major. My first job was working at Subway. Although it was a fast food chain, I was provided opportunities to climb up the management ladder. Eventually, after about six months, I got the promotion to become a shift lead. Like previously mentioned, although lacking the professional atmosphere, having the opportunity to hold responsibility at a young age taught me various things about hard work and accountability. I applied these disciplines into my studies; this led me to begin my student career at The University of California, Riverside.

Beginning my student career was far from easy, even the beginning stages proved themselves to be extremely difficult. Everything from the college application to the FAFSA form confused me as a first generation student. I was first accepted for a psychology major, but as time progressed I came to figure out that I truly wanted to pursue a degree in business. The major change was difficult, as I had to petition to take ARC35 and complete it. Now that I am pursuing the career that I flourish in, I have realized the attributes that I can bring to a professional setting. Due to all the circumstances I have experienced getting here, I have gained and refined professional characteristics I used to have. Working in customer service allowed me to gain the ability to properly communicate with a customer in order to sell the product in the most efficient way. The opportunity to interact with people everyday allowed me to meet people with different personalities; this taught me to hold my ground in a respectful and business like manner

Similarly, being a first generation student taught me to be resourceful and persistent. There were many times in which I wanted to simply give up. However, instead I learned to be determined and searched for different ways to solve my problems. This was brought into my student career. By being determined I was able to change my major despite the issues that were presented. My career at The University of California, Riverside has been an enjoyable rollercoaster, and as a result I am very grateful for the fact that I refused to give up. Outside of a professional and student setting, I have experience as a volunteer. I was a part of a program called C5, for 6 years. Being a part of this program was doing community service such as the beautification and clean up of nature. We also toured to multiple schools and presented information to other low-income students. C5 was an internship of shorts; I had the opportunity to meet with professionals of professions that range from multiple business careers, such as: corporate owners, non-profit organizations, and independent contractors. All in all, my life, professional, and student experience has prepared me for a career in business.
